Space math: global warming trends

1 *Data* A combination of hundreds of climate studies have traced changes in the average temperature of Earth since before the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_Revolution,Industrial Revolution]. The plot below shows the changes since 1880 info.
The global mean land-ocean temperature is shown on the vertical axis. The temperture is *relative* to the average temperature for the period 1951-1980. Time is shown in two equivalent horizontal axes. The upper axis is simply the date in years. The lower axis is the time *relative to 1960* in decades. To dig into the raw data see [b00hq,this blab].
2 *Model* The temperature after 1960 can be modelled by a line $T(x) = ax + b$, where $x$ is the number of *decades* since 1960. In the plot you can drag the green circles to try different slopes ($a$) and intercepts ($b$).
